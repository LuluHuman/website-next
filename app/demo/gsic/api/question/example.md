To sustainably dispose of a failed SSD, do not throw it in your regular trash as it is considered electronic waste (e-waste) containing valuable, recyclable components and potentially hazardous materials. Before disposal, try to securely erase your data if the drive still powers on; if it is completely unresponsive, you may choose to physically destroy the storage chips for added security. For proper recycling, take the drive to a certified e-waste collection center, a participating electronics retailer with a recycling program, or check Western Digital’s website for a manufacturer take-back or trade-in program. These specialized facilities ensure that metals and plastics are recovered and repurposed, keeping them out of landfills.
